Spaghetti all'Assassina(Assassin's Spaghetti)

This recipe is quick and easy to make and will leave your mouth watering for more.
This dish will add a little spice to your life(if you're feeling spicy that is) with this partially panfried, savory dish.
The pan frying giving it a unique texture and in the right pan for the job and you have an easy family dish for 4 ready in 30 minutes.
The carmelized sauce making it delectable.

Ingredients

Tomato broth:

  • 1 Tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups tomato puree
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste

Assassin's Spaghetti

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons red chili flakes,or to taste
  • 6 onces dry spaghetti
  • salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped parsley, for garnish

Steps

  1. Gather all of the needed ingredients
  2. Heat olive oil in a pot over medium heat. Add garlic and cook until it becomes fragrant and the color becomes golden, about 1 minute.
    Stir in tomato puree, water, and salt.Bring to a simmer on medium-high heat. Once simmering, reduce to low heat and keep warm.
  3. Pour olive oil into a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat, season with chili flakes(if desired) until they start to sizzle, for 1 minute.
    Add raw spaghetti and toss until well coated with chili oil(you can add different seasonings for flavor besides spice).
  4. Pour in about 3 cups of tomato broth, then with tongs move the pasta from side to side to evenly distribute the tomato broth.
    Cook, occasionally moving the pasta from side to side with tongs, keep the noodles facing the same direction, until most of the broth has been absorbed or evaporated.
    The noodles will start to fry, allow it.
  5. Turn spaghetti over with tongs, spread evenly in the pan and allow the pasta to brown or even lightly char.
  6. Add about 2 more cups of the tomato broth, and repeat the process(move back and fourth with tongs, absorb, spread, brown or char).
    Cook to your desired doneness and if you want your dish saucy you can add more broth or relatively dry.
