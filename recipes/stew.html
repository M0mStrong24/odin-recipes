Viking Stew


This stew seems primitive with its, fresh herbs, root vegetables, berries, mushrooms, and meat. All things
available to the Vikings with earthy flavor and works with any meats so don't be discouraged if you can't
find a wild boar, the dish will still be delicious.

Ingredients

  • 4 1/2 pounds of boneless pork shoulder roast, cut into 2-to-3 inch chunks
  • 3 teaspoons kosher salt, or to taste
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 cup fresh blackberries or other fresh berries
  • 2 tablespoon lingonberry jam
  • 6 cups cold water
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 yellow onion, cut into large dice
  • 2 leeks, washed thoroughly, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 10 large brown mushrooms, halved
  • 1 large green cabage, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 3 large carrots, peeled and cut into 1/4-inch strips
  • 2 fennel bulbs, halved and cut into 1/4-inch strips
  • 1/4 cup torn cilantro leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves

Directions

  1. Add pork(or choice of meat) to a large soup pot or Dutch oven. Add salt, honey, berries, lingonberry jam,
    and cold water, and turn the heaet to high.
  2. While stew is coming to a boil, add garlic, dry thyme, and ground coriander. When mixture comes to a
    rolling boil, reduce heat to los, and simmer gently for 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Stir in leeks, mushrooms, and simmer until meat is almost, but not quite fork tneder, about 45 more minutes.
  4. Stir in cabbage, carrots, and fennel; contine to simmer until vegetables and meat are very tender, about 30 minutes
  5. Season to taste with salt. Serve with fresh herbs scattered over the top.
